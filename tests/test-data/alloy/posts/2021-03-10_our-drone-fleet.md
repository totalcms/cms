We have 3 Drones, which - in conjunction with my CAA-certification - allows us to legally fly in most locations. 2 of them are capable of shooting in full 4K resolution, and the third is even designed for indoor flight!
<br><br>

### DJI Mavic Air 2
![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/DJIMavicAir2.jpg)
Such a stable platform for getting superb photos and video footage. The 4K camera is really sharp, although most applications only require 1080p resolution, meaning we can film at higher frame rates, allowing fabulous slow-motion shots. Although having the capability to apply a slew of in-camera effects, we prefer to stick to the raw footage, and do the magic in post-production. *Drogon* (Game of Thrones reference!) weighs in at 570g, which restricts us from using it in heavily built-up areas, now that the new 2021 Drone laws are in place, which is why we have... 
<br><br>

### DJI Mini 2
![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/DJIMini2.jpg)
Weighing in officially at under 249g (we've weighed *Rhaegal* at 236g!), means that there are no restrictions on where we can fly - apart from CAA No-Fly Zones - we've already flown in Central London, although in this instance we only needed to go up to 23m, rather than the legal ceiling of 120m. Another aircraft with 4K capability, and remarkably stable flight characteristics, resulting in great footage to enhance any production.
<br><br>

### Ryze Tello
![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/RyzeTello.jpg)
80g. EIGHTY GRAMS. Including the Battery! Is it a toy? Yes, and no. It's designed to fly indoors, and outside up to 30m high. *Tyrion* (what did you expect!), has a 720p camera, and can be flown pretty much anywhere we aren't able to launch the other 2 Drones.
<br><br>

### DJI Smart Controller
![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/DJISmartController.jpg)
We mainly use the Smart Controller with the Mavic Air 2. It saves having to use a mobile phone, attached to a bulky controller, as it has a really bright Android-powered tablet built in. Having a clear view of everything the aircraft is seeing, as well as a complete range of situation readouts, makes for safer flights.
