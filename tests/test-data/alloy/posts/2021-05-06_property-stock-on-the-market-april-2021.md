Last month we wrote a piece that looked at the percentage of the total property stock that was on the market in selected areas. A month on we’ve had another look to see if anything has changed, and it’s led to some interesting discoveries.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/April2-1x.jpg)

Generally speaking, the number of properties on the market has dropped in the last month.  The dip is only 0.2% in London, but elsewhere it ranges from 4% to 13%.  Property certainly seems to be selling, apparently more quickly than new properties are being listed.

As before, we also looked at the percentage of property on the market that’s priced over £2m, and this brought another surprising result.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/April2-2x.jpg)

In virtually every area we’ve looked at, there are more properties on the market over £2m now, than there were at this time last month.  It looks like this part of the market is getting more competitive, with more choice for buyers.

We only have data to look at a fuller range of prices in London, but that does reinforce the observation that the choice for buyers at lower budgets has decreased in the last month, whilst at higher budgets, the market has become more competitive. The job of selling these homes has got that little bit harder, so making your home stand out has never been more important.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/April2-3x.jpg)

Although not directly related to our videography interests, these are fascinating areas so we’ll keep an eye on these stats and report any other interesting observations.

*Sources: Zoopla and ONS*
