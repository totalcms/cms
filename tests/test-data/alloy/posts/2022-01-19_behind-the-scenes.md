TWO DAYS to get ONE IMAGE!

Wait...WHAT?!

The brief was simple. Three pewter measures, against an interesting (but not distracting) backdrop, floating in mid-air(!), with liquid pouring from one, to another, to another.

How hard could it be?

Well, the Behind the Scenes image (taken on an iPhone), below, shows where we ended up to capture the shot. Actually, technically there are three shots that comprise the final image. One of the backdrop alone, one with the measures clamped in place, and one (selected from more than 200!) with the measures still in place, and the liquid pouring.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Pewter_Measures_BTS.jpeg)

A lot of equipment is required to capture a final image like this, as you can see. There's a degree of trial and error, since every product has different properties of reflectivity, and pouring liquid is unpredictable to say the least. Apart from the multitude of stands, brackets, reflectors and flags required, we used our studio strobe, and two speedlites (with wireless triggers) to get just the right amount of light.

We recently invested in *Capture One* software, which we're using on a MacBook Pro to 'tether' the camera via a cable. This allows us to make adjustments easily, whilst being able to view shots instantly - it's a real game-changer - it even allows multiple people to view the 'live' images, using a companion app on an iPad!

With the images "in the can", it was time to move over to our editing suite - a top-of-the-range Apple iMac, running *Photoshop* and *Lightroom*, among others - where the images were composited together, to get the best light, the best liquid pour, and to remove the clamps.

We're absolutely delighted with the final image, and we hope you like it, too!

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Pewter_Measures-1280.jpg)
