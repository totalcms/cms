There are two things that can absorb me for hours and hours, to the point that I forget to eat!  Editing videos and researching history.  So, when we video an historic house, I love to research its past and make an edit that explores how the property has been used over the years, who made it their home, and how it sits in its surroundings.

When I started to research the history of the Barkham Terrace Townhouse we filmed a few weeks ago, I was amazed at the detail I could find.  It was built nearly 180 years ago, and has been both a family home and part of a hospital, whilst the area around it also has some stories to tell.  Before the Imperial War Museum, the area across the road was the Bedlam Hospital, a school for the blind, and in the 18th Century, a very fashionable but controversial tavern!
