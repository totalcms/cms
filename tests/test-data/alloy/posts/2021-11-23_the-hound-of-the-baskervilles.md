Wistman's Wood, on Dartmoor, could well have inspired Conan Doyle, but we wanted to try out some light painting, and we're made of stern stuff, so we weren't going to let legends of Hell Hounds stop us! Where better than an ancient wood, tucked deep in the moor, on a misty, drizzly night, in November? Oh, and a full moon.

> **“The setting is a worthy one, if the devil did desire to have a hand in the affairs of men.” **<br>
> *― Arthur Conan Doyle, The Hound of the Baskervilles*

Armed with cameras, tripods and an extremely powerful torch - not to mention head torches and mobile phones for backup - we set off on the walk to the wood. It was still daylight, but we only saw two other people on the way.

What a stunning place! Like nothing I've ever seen before. The oaks' tangled branches and the uneven rocky floor make it impossible for Dartmoor ponies and cattle to enter. This is the main reason why the wood still exists on the moor - its inaccessibility keeps it protected from destructive grazing.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/521A6978-1.jpg)

And then night fell. One of the benefits of shooting in November is that the sun set at 4:30pm. We had an absolute blast! Long (2 and 3 minute) 'Bulb' exposures, lighting the trees and moss-covered rocks with the torch, to get some fabulously spooky shots.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/521A6994b-2.jpg)

Truly magical!

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/521A6997-2_0.jpg)

I have to admit that the walk back to the car was a little bit fraught, but the plethora of torches certainly helped with the terrain. I suspect that my repeated Holmes reference to 'Grimpen Mire' every time we came across a puddle might have worn a bit thin, but it passed the time, and distracted us from thoughts of Werewolves...

Back in the car park, there were a few people with dogs, so we did see a hound, but we didn't ask anyone for their surname, hence the "(probably)".
