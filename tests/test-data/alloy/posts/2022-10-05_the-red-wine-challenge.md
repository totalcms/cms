No, not a drinking contest - although we *do* love a bottle of Red - but successfully photographing it is a real challenge!
 
The main issues are that it's almost impossible to accurately show the colour of the wine through the bottle, and unless you know what you're doing, the reflections in the bottle (and glass) can be ugly, to say the least.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Wine_Majestica-800.jpg)

We're very happy with the results we got, although the setup was pretty complex, using multiple studio strobes in soft-boxes, firing through Lee 216 diffusion material, at an angle suitable to achieve a lovely, graduated light around the bottle. We then added a further strobe, with a coloured gel, in order to give the subtle hint of red. There's some other special techniques and equipment used, but we can't give away *all* our tricks, can we?

Finally, the glass was photographed separately. Why? So that we could avoid the glass and the bottle reflecting on each other, or casting a shadow. Then everything was 'comped' together, 'cleaned' and 'denoised', to produce the final images you see, below.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Wine_Guvnor-800.jpg)

It's quite the painstaking process, but we're perfectionists. Case-in-point, we also shot a vintage bottle of St Emilion, which had to be carefully cleaned - not overdone, so as to properly depict its age.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Wine_St_Emilion-800.jpg)
