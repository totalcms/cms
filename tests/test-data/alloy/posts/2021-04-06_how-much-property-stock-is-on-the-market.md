When we researched our [article](https://ktbb.co.uk/blog/video-assets-in-new-london-listings-march-2021) on new London property listings, we discovered 850 new listings over £2mil in March, which got us thinking... *Are there really a huge number of London homes for sale, or are there just a lot of London homes?*

So, we did a bit of research on the percentage of the total property stock on the market, in different areas.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/2103_-_Percentage_of_Housing_Stock_for_sale.png)
*(Sources: Zoopla and ONS)*

Our first discovery was just how HUGE the London housing stock actually is!  How can so many homes be so expensive?  London truly is a strange and wonderful place.  But the percentage of homes up for sale isn’t especially high, compared with surrounding areas.  There isn’t a sudden London exodus, as far as we can see.

That said, it does look as if there is generally more of the home counties on the market than other areas, so perhaps there is a tendency for people to move out, as people learn they can work from home.

We’ve also had a look at the percentage of property on the market, priced over £2m, just for the fun of it!

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/2103_-_Listings_over_2m.png)
*(Source: Zoopla)*

We were quite surprised by this, as we didn’t expect Buckinghamshire to be number two in the chart, or Kent to be relatively low. 

Although not directly related to our videography interests, these are fascinating areas, so we’ll keep an eye on these stats and review them monthly.
