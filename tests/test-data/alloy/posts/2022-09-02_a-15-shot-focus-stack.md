FIFTEEN individual shots! For a pen! The beautifully ornate details on this Venetian Pen needed to be photographed close up.

Great. How hard can it be?

Well... As a Photographer, it's imperative to understand 'Depth-of-Field' (DoF), which is the distance between the nearest and furthest elements in a scene that appear to be acceptably sharp in an image. There are a number of factors that affect DoF, including 'Focal Length' and the distance to the subject, amongst others, so it's pretty tricky...

When shooting so close to the pen, the DoF can be measured in millimeters!

We knew we only wanted the pen in focus, with the vintage notebook nicely blurred - naturally, the product needs to be the Hero. We calculated that we'd need 15 individual shots, each with a slightly different focal point, in order to capture enough to use a technique, known as 'Focus Stacking', into the final image.
