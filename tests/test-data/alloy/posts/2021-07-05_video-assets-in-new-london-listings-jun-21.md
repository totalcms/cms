This continues a regular monthly review of new listings in London and how video and 3D walkthroughs are being used in a competitive property market.  To provide a little comparison, we’ve also included figures in surrey, and an overview of the total £2m plus market in selected other areas.

In June 2021, there were 786 new £2m plus property listings in London, down 7% on the 844 added in April. This figure has been relatively stable since we began monitoring in March.  

In Surrey, only 81 new properties over £2m have been listed, down by just one from the 82 in the previous month.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/June_Graph_1.png)

In London, the total number of listing over £2million at the end of the month was 5,041, down by 29, less than 1%, on the position at the end of May.  815 properties in this price range either sold or were taken off the market during the month, which is up by 10% on the previous month.  Combined with a drop in the number of new properties, this has led to the slight dip in available properties.

In Surrey, the total on the market in that price range at the end of the month was 338, compared with 331 the previous month.  So, in June, the market has grown by just 7 properties.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/June_Graph_2.png)

The growth in this high-end stock has checked during June, but it’s still a hugely competitive market and the need to make listings stand out remains.

In London, the average number of photos per new listing is 16.3, which is not consistent with previous months. In Surrey, the average number is 21.6, which is also consistent over time.  The figure is higher than London, but we believe this simply reflects the larger properties outside of London.  

The number of working videos on the new London listings is only 42 (5.3%), slightly up on the previous month when the figure was 37 (4.4%).  

However, in Surrey, the use of videos has reversed its positive trend.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/June_Graph_3.png)

When it comes to 3D walkthroughs, the numbers are a little better in London, at 48, but this is also down on the 51 we saw in April.  There was a tiny increase of 1 in Surrey.  We believe these are being used less now property viewings are less daunting.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/June_Graph_4.png)

We’ve also had a look at the usage of these assets in a few other parts of the country.  As numbers are much lower, we’ve looked at the total number of properties on the market in the price range, rather than new listings. We’ve included the total £2m plus market in Surrey, for comparison.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/June_Graph_5.png)

In June, the total size of the £2m market has grown a little in Surrey, but in all other areas researched, it has shrunk slightly.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/June_Graph_6.png)

The use of videos remains erratic in most areas, with low numbers vulnerable to fluctuation.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/June_Graph_7.png)

There is little sign of significant change in the use of these assets, which don’t seem to have been as widely adopted outside of London.  Devon percentages seem to be consistently higher, but numbers are low so remain statistically vulnerable.  It’ll be interesting to see how things evolve over the coming months.

**Why does all this matter?**

In all forms of marketing, video is proven to significantly increase engagement.  It’s more likely to hold attention, will improve your search engine rankings and is much more likely to be shared.  Research has indicated nearly three quarters of property sellers would favour an agent that uses video.  And industry stats have suggested a listing with a video gets over four times as many enquiries as one without.  Some reports in the US claim that figure is ten times!
