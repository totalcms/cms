It's a good question.

Well, no two requirements are the same. Every project we undertake is different, and that's a good thing - you don't want your products to look identical to everybody else's, do you?

It's relatively straightforward to produce an image like these scones on a white background (once you know how to make sure white is white, without corrupting the product's colours, of course). It's unremarkable - nothing fancy, no props, *no context* - but fine for an e-commerce site, if a little dull.
 
![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/White_Scones_800.jpg)

Unfortunately, it doesn't really differentiate you from your competition. For that, we need to add some context. We need to make the image *interesting*. That may be by adding props, by changing the surface or backdrop, or by adding a lovely summery light to the composition, such as we did with this cream doughnut. Warm, bright and delicious!

Clearly, this type of setup takes longer to perfect than the scones. Lighting needs to be carefully adjusted, and would need to be readjusted if, say, we then need to photograph a loaf of bread in a similar style. The props we used are subtle, but need to be carefully sourced so that they fit the image correctly...

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Cream_Doughnut_800.jpg)

And then we get to the photo of the smoked salmon roll.

*Believe it or not, the product here is the bread roll!* 

But this is a "Hero shot". For advertising, for a website banner, for point-of-sale, so it needs to be special. It needs to be classy. It needs to make you *want* it.

That's where we go to the next level, bringing our food styling skills to the party, along with some 'tricks of the trade'. It's a one-off shot, so the lighting is bespoke to give a luxurious feel. There are more props, to give more context. Even the condensation on the glass of orange juice has been styled. We also subtly played around with the depth-of-field, to maximise impact.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Smoked_Salmon_Roll_800.jpg)

The point here is that each of these shots took a different amount of time, effort and expertise. If we publish a tiered indicative price list, there's a real risk that a client could budget for a plain white scone e-commerce shoot, and produce a mood board for smoked salmon rolls.

That kind of miscommunication is exactly what we try to avoid - it just wastes everybody's time. It's why we take the time to meet (virtually, if necessary), so we can discuss your requirement with you, face to face. That way, we ensure 100% alignment between your vision and our quotation, and everyone's expectations are properly managed.

Why not [give us a shout](https://ktbb.co.uk/contact). We're not the cheapest (quality costs, right?), although what we quote you will be what you pay - no hidden costs with us - and we're far from the most expensive options out there. We think you'll love what we can do to make your products really stand out from the crowd!
