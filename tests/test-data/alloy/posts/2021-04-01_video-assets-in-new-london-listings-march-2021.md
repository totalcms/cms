This is the first of a regular monthly review of new listings in London and how video and 3D walkthroughs are being used in a competitive property market.

During March 2021, there were 850 new property listings in London, priced over £2million!

That’s not the total number of listing over £2million – that figure’s 4,818 – that’s just new listings in March! *(Source: Zoopla)*

Whichever way you look at that, it’s one competitive market!  So how are estate agents working to make sure their properties stand out?

On average, these homes have 16 photos.  Now this is London, so a £2million property may not be huge, but that feels low, doesn’t it?  Perhaps you don’t want to give everything away - after all you want people to see enough to make them book a viewing.  But is that argument valid in the current environment?  We’re not sure.

Shockingly, 50 of these listings don’t even include a floorplan!

What about videos?  Surprisingly, only 59 of these properties have videos, and in 6 of those the video link doesn’t actually work!  So just 6.2% of properties listed over £2million have a video.  

Slightly more (63 – 7.4%) have a 3D walkthrough, allowing potential customers to explore the property remotely. These reveal the whole of the property, so if you’re doing fewer photos so you leave something to come and see, these potentially undermine that!  But they’re a practical way of using technology to show off a home and we believe they have a valuable part to play in the marketing pack.

But if you think these figures seem low, compared with areas outside of London, they’re really rather good!

In the same period, in the same price range, 105 properties have been listed in Surrey.  The average number of photos is higher, at 21, but there are only four videos (3.8%) and two (1.9%) 3D walkthroughs.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/New_Listings_over_2m.png)
*(Source: Zoopla)*

So, in the busier London market, more estate agents are using video and 3D walkthroughs, but the penetration of both is still low.

**Why does this matter?**

In all forms of marketing, video is *proven* to increase engagement significantly.  It’s more likely to hold attention, will improve your search engine rankings and is much more likely to be shared.  Research has suggested nearly three quarters of property sellers would favour an agent that uses video.  And industry stats have suggested a listing with a video gets over four times as many enquiries as one without.  Some reports in the US claim that figure is ten times!
