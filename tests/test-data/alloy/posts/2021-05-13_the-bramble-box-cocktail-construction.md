A new start-up home-based cocktail business recently asked us to produce a concept instruction video to accompany their prototype launch product. The brief was clear: Make it accessible, relatable, warm and fun!

We were only too happy to work with them to deliver what they needed – What’s not to like? Making cocktails and filming it…

We set up two cameras and our studio lighting for multiple angles and got to work. The Bramble Box was well packaged, containing 5 bottles, a measuring beaker, and dehydrated lemon slices – all we needed was a cocktail shaker, glasses, and some ice. I was particularly intrigued by the bottle of “shrub” (sweetened fruit-flavoured vinegar, in case you’re wondering).

The written instructions were clear, but it’s a great idea to also provide visual step-by-step direction, allowing customers to choose whichever is more convenient for them – it's inclusive, and it’s probably easier to follow when you’re on your fourth round…

Full disclosure: I don’t usually like Gin, but this was delicious – we had to make another round (just to make sure we had plenty of footage to work from, obviously!
