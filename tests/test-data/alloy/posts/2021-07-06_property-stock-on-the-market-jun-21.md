Continuing our monthly exploration of the number of properties on the market in selected areas, our June review has reinforced the trends we’ve seen over the last couple of months.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Market_June_Graph_1.png)

The total number of properties on the market continues to fall. Across the areas we’re monitoring, the available stock has now fallen by 9% since March, which must be felt by those shopping for a new home.

We’ve again looked at the percentage of property on the market that’s priced over £2m, and this has continued to be interesting.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Market_June_Graph_2.png)

The % of the market priced over £2m has continued to increase.  We’ve looked in more detail in Surrey and London, and the rate at which new properties are being added has slowed somewhat, resulting in a more stable market size.  So, that balance must be different for lower price properties, where it must be getting increasingly difficult to find the property you’re looking for.

We have data to look at a fuller range of prices in London.   That reveals that property stock on the market is now falling at all price points, but is far more pronounced at lower levels.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Market_June_Graph_3.png)

Although not directly related to our videography interests, these are fascinating areas so we’ll keep an eye on these stats and report any other interesting observations.

*Sources: Zoopla and ONS*
