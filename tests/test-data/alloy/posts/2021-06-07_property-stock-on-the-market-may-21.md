Continuing our monthly exploration of the number of properties on the market in selected areas, our May review has again found some interesting trends.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Market_May_Graph_1.png)

The total number of properties on the market continues to fall in most areas. Across the areas we’re monitoring, the available stock has fallen by 6% since March.

As before, we’ve also looked at the percentage of property on the market that’s priced over £2m, and this brought more unexpected results.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Market_May_Graph_2.png)

In most areas we’ve looked at, the % of the market priced over £2m has continued to increase.  We’ve looked in more detail in Surrey and London, and new properties being added at the price point have slightly exceeded those being sold, but they are definitely selling.  However, that balance is clearly different for lower price properties.

We only have data to look at a fuller range of prices in London, but that does reinforce the observation that the choice for buyers at lower budgets is decreasing month on month, whilst at higher budgets, the market has become more competitive. The job of selling these homes has got that little bit harder, so making your home stand out has never been more important.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Market_May_Graph_3.png)

Although not directly related to our videography interests, these are fascinating areas so we’ll keep an eye on these stats and report any other interesting observations.

*Sources: Zoopla and ONS*
