This continues a regular monthly review of new listings in London and how video and 3D walkthroughs are being used in a competitive property market.

During April 2021, there were 830 new property listings in London, priced over £2million, slightly down on the 850 added in March. 

The total number of listings over £2million at the end of the month was 4,967, up by 149 on the position at the end of March.  So, 681 properties in this price range either sold or were taken off the market during the month.  As this doesn’t balance the new-to-market figure, the stock available has grown by 3% in a month. More choice for buyers – more competition for sellers and their agents.

**How are estate agents using listings to make their properties stand out?**

The average number of photos per new listing is very slightly lower in April, at 15.7 compared with 16.4 in March. 

64 new listings don’t have a floorplan, representing 8% of the total.  That’s up from 50 (6%) in March.  Surely a floorplan is important when you’re in the early stages of property research?

The number of working videos on these new listings is also slightly down on the previous month, with 46 (5.5%) compared with 53 (6.2%) in March. 

Once again, more have a 3D walkthrough (64 – 7.7%), allowing potential customers to explore the property remotely. This is also the only figure that’s increased slightly since last month, when 63 (7.4%) of the new listings had this feature.  Are there initial signs of a trend towards these 3D representations?

**How does the specialist London market compare with other areas?**

In April, 107 properties over £2m have been listed in Surrey, up from 105 in the previous month.  The total on the market in that price range at the end of the month was 321, compared with 271 the previous month.  As in London, more have been added than removed, making the market more competitive.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/April1x.jpg)

The average number of photos on new listings remains higher than in London, at 20.9, exactly the same as in the previous month.  This is likely to simply reflect the larger properties outside of London.

Although numbers are low, there is an improvement in the number of videos on these new Surrey listings.  In March only 4 (4%) had a video, but that’s increased to 7 (6.5%) in April and is now ever so slightly higher than the London rate of 5.5%.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/April2x.jpg)

The number of 3D walkthroughs has also improved in the area, with 4 (3.7%) compared with 2 (1.9%) in March.  Although rates remain behind those seen in London, and numbers are very low, there may be early indicators of an increase in popularity for these.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/April3x.jpg)

We’ve also had a look at the usage of these assets in a few other parts of the country.  As numbers are much lower, we’ve looked at the total number of properties in the market in the price range, rather than new listings.  We’ve included Surrey, for comparison.

Although this section of the market has grown in three of the four areas we’ve focused on, the use of videos and 3D walkthroughs has only increased in one for each.

**Why does this matter?**

In all forms of marketing, video is proven to significantly increase engagement.  It’s more likely to hold attention, will improve your search engine rankings and is much more likely to be shared.  Research has indicated nearly three quarters of property sellers would favour an agent that uses video.  And industry stats have suggested a listing with a video gets over four times as many enquiries as one without.  Some reports in the US claim that figure is ten times!
