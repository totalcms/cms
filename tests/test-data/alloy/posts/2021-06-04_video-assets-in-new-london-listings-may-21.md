This continues a regular monthly review of new listings in London and how video and 3D walkthroughs are being used in a competitive property market.  To provide a little comparison, we’ve also included figures in Surrey, and an overview of the total £2m plus market in selected other areas.

In May 2021, there were 844 new £2m plus property listings in London, slightly up on the 830 added in April. This figure has been relatively stable since we began monitoring in March.  

In Surrey, only 81 new properties over £2m have been listed, down 24% from 107 in the previous month.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/May_Graph_1.png)

In London, the total number of listing over £2million at the end of the month was 5,070, up by 103 (2%) on the position at the end of April.  So, 742 properties in this price range either sold or were taken off the market during the month, which is up by 9% on the previous month.  But the 2% growth shows that sales are still not quite balancing the new-to-market figure, and available stock is still increasing.  

In Surrey, the total on the market in that price range at the end of the month was 331, compared with 321 the previous month.  So, in May, the market has grown by 10 properties, much lower than the April figure of 50.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/May_Graph_2.png)

Even in the smaller Surrey market, the £2m plus market is increasingly competitive.  How are estate agents using listings to make their properties stand out?

In London, the average number of photos per new listing is 16.1, compared with 15.7 in April and 16.4 in March. In Surrey, the average number is 21.2, marginally up on the previous month.   The figure is higher than London, but we believe this simply reflects the larger properties outside of London.  There is very little fluctuation in this metric.

In London, 63 new listings don’t have a floorplan, representing 7.5% of the total.  Again, there is very little change compared with the 8% in April, but it still surprises us that so many listings don’t have this feature!  In Surrey, the figure sits at 2 out of the 81 new listings – 2.5%.  Well done, Surrey Estate Agents! 

The number of working videos on the new London listings is only 37 (4.4%), noticeably down on the previous month when the figure was 5.5%, and 6.2% in March.  We’ve already seen that the market is becoming more competitive for vendors, so a drop in the number of supporting assets is surprising.

In Surrey, the use of videos is increasing.  Hopefully this is a positive trend that will continue, but with numbers so low this could be a false dawn!

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/May_Graph_3.png)

When it comes to 3D walkthroughs, the number are a little better in London, at 51, but this is also down on the 64 we saw in April.  There is also a decrease in Surrey.  Last month we asked if there was a trend towards 3D walkthroughs, but this month’s figures suggest that trend, if it exists, may have stalled.  Perhaps these are seen as less important now real-world viewings have become easier?

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/May_Graph_4.png)

We’ve also had a look at the usage of these assets in a few other parts of the country.  As numbers are much lower, we’ve looked at the total number of properties on the market in the price range, rather than new listings.  We’ve included the total £2m plus market in Surrey, for comparison.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/May_Graph_5.png)

In the last month, there has been a notable growth in the £2m market in all four areas we’ve researched.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/May_Graph_6.png)

The use of videos has also increased in three of the four areas, with only Surrey showing a decrease.  

Interestingly, this decrease is only in the total listings – the number included in new listings has increased.  So, this suggests that properties with videos have sold at a higher rate than those without - this is absolutely what we’d expect to see!

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/May_Graph_7.png)

There are small signs that 3D walkthroughs are increasing in popularity, but the numbers are small so may not be meaningful.  We’ll keep watching!

**Why does all this matter?**

In all forms of marketing, video is proven to significantly increase engagement.  It’s more likely to hold attention, will improve your search engine rankings and is much more likely to be shared.  Research has indicated nearly three quarters of property sellers would favour an agent that uses video.  And industry stats have suggested a listing with a video gets over four times as many enquiries as one without.  Some reports in the US claim that figure is ten times!
