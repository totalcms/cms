I wanted to play around artistically with High Dynamic Range (HDR). Some Photographers take a purist's view of what the final photo should look like, but I think that there's a place for enhancing an image, to make it more striking.

This image of Rochester Castle really benefits from a more dramatic, monochrome edit. There's no composite, and the sky hasn't been replaced. Just by selectively tweaking the various exposure settings, such as Contrast and Saturation, we now have a fabulously moody photo, and I love the sunrays through the clouds.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/HDR_Rochester_1280.jpg)

If you know where to go in the City of London, there are some streets and alleys that still look like they did in 1888 - just add fog, and it's easy to imagine Jack the Ripper being round the next corner! I really liked this image, but there was an air conditioning vent, which somewhat ruined the effect, so that needed to be digitally removed, and the sepia tone really ages it nicely.

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/HDR_City_1280.jpg)

Some images are just screaming out to be given an extreme edit. Extreme HDR can take a beautiful image and turn it into a piece of art, with colours that really leap off the canvas. I must admit, I'm a little bit in love with how this long-exposure sunset image of Mortehoe in Devon turned out!
