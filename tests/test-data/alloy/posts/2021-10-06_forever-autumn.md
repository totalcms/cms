Ever since I was a kid I’ve loved Jeff Wayne’s The War of the Worlds, and Forever Autumn in particular.  It has always conjured moody images of London on a wild autumnal day.  Fabulous stuff.  

So, when The War of the Worlds Experience invited entries for a competition based on the song, I couldn’t wait to get creative.  (As an aside, The War of the Worlds Experience is INCREDIBLE!  We’ve done it twice and wouldn’t rule out a return!)

We took this photo of Tower Bridge a couple of years ago and it seemed perfect as the basis for some art. After hours of work, there followed a small panic concerning dates, but the bridge was finished in 1896 and The War of the worlds was published in 1897!  It refers to ‘the final years of the 19th Century’, so I think we’re OK.  The bridge was brand spanking new, so the threat of its destruction must have been particularly distressing!

![](https://ktbb.co.uk/image-uploads/Forever_Autumn-1280_No_Watermark.jpg)

This was a new style of work for me, but I’m really pleased with the outcome.  Is it photography or art?  I’m sure this subject will cause conflict on photographic Facebook groups long after all technology takes the next leap forward!  I think of it as photographic art and I’m happy to use whatever tools are available to make a beautiful image.

Maybe this image could win and Jeff Wayne himself will choose my work!  But whatever the outcome, this was great fun to make.
